What is a Patent?

Understanding intellectual property rights and how patents protect your innovations

Protection

A patent is an exclusive right granted for an invention, providing the patent owner with protection for their creation. It prevents others from making, using, or selling the invention without permission.

Innovation

Patents encourage innovation by giving inventors recognition and potential financial benefits for their creative solutions. They promote the sharing of knowledge while protecting the inventor's rights.

Duration

Design patents typically last for 10 years from the filing date, giving inventors a significant period of exclusive rights to benefit from their designs and innovations.

Recognition

Having a patent adds credibility to your innovation. It's recognized globally as a mark of genuine invention and can open doors to business opportunities and collaborations.

What is a Design Patent?

A design patent protects the ornamental appearance of a functional item. It covers the unique visual qualities of a product, including its shape, configuration, pattern, or ornamentation.

Unlike utility patents that protect how something works, design patents protect how something looks. This makes them perfect for innovative product designs, user interfaces, and creative visual solutions.

  • Protects the visual design and aesthetics
  • Covers shape, pattern, and ornamentation
  • Faster and more affordable than utility patents
  • Perfect for product and UI designs
